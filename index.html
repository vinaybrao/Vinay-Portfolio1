Rapido Wanderlust

A product growth case study exploring how Rapido can increase ride frequency by transforming from a pure transportation utility into a city discovery companion — creating new demand, not just serving existing demand.

1
India's leading bike-taxi platform
Low
User switching costs vs. Ola & Uber
3mo
Discovery-to-MVP timeline
The Problem
Most users open Rapido only when they already know where to go. The app acts as a transportation utility rather than a discovery platform, limiting engagement and creating heavy dependence on discounts to drive rides.
Goal
Can Rapido create ride demand instead of only serving existing demand — without relying on discounts and promotions?
4
MVP features
3
Growth phases
Project Overview
From transportation utility to city exploration companion.
01
Business Context
Rapido operates across Bike Taxi, Auto, Cab, and Delivery and competes directly with Ola and Uber in a market where user loyalty is low and switching costs are minimal.
  • Acquisition growing but ride frequency plateauing
  • High dependence on promotions to drive bookings
  • Limited reasons for users to open the app between rides
02
Research Approach
I explored user personas, travel behaviors, city exploration habits, ride booking journeys, and mobility pain points to surface unmet needs beyond standard commuting.
  • Persona analysis across 3 key user types
  • Discovery behavior patterns among new city residents
  • Opportunity sizing for intent-driven ride creation
03
The Opportunity
Users don't always begin with transportation — they begin with intent. "I want to explore Bangalore." "I have a free evening." Transportation is simply the bridge between intent and destination.
  • New city residents & students unfamiliar with local attractions
  • Tourists with no discovery layer in ride-hailing apps
  • Young professionals with discretionary free time
User Personas
Three users. Three unmet needs beyond the ride.
Persona analysis revealed a consistent pattern — many users lacked local discovery, not transportation access.
P
Priya, 23
The Explorer · Student
Needs
Affordable travel, safety, and recommendations for places to visit in the city.
⚡ Pain: Doesn't know what places to visit, worries about safety, gets hit by surge pricing.
S
Shivam, 25
The Working Professional · Engineer
Needs
Local discovery, convenience, and reliable transportation after relocating to a new city.
⚡ Pain: Unfamiliar with the city, faces language barriers, and worries about hidden costs.
R
Rajesh, 32
The Busy Commuter · Small Business Owner
Needs
Fast transport, predictable pricing, and minimal wait times for frequent city travel.
⚡ Pain: Ride cancellations, long wait times, and traffic delays reduce trust and frequency.
Key Insight
Intent comes before transportation.
01
Users don't begin with transportation. They begin with intent — "I want to explore Bangalore" or "I have a free evening." Rapido can intercept that intent.
02
New city residents, tourists, and students struggle with local discovery — they don't know what to visit, how far it is, or how much the ride will cost.
03
Competitors focus entirely on rides. No ride-hailing platform has built a discovery layer that creates ride demand — this is an open, defensible differentiation opportunity.
04
Fare transparency at the discovery stage — showing distance, ride time, and estimated cost before booking — dramatically reduces booking friction for exploratory trips.
Proposed Solution
Wanderlust — "Where every ride becomes an adventure."
A destination discovery experience built directly into Rapido. Instead of simply booking rides, users can discover places worth visiting nearby — then book instantly from the attraction card.
Flagship · Feature 01
Destination Recommendations
Proactive discovery within the Rapido home screen
A personalized "Wanderlust" section surfaces popular attractions and hidden gems based on the user's current location, city, interests, and seasonal trends. Each destination card includes photos, distance, visiting hours, and estimated fare — giving users everything they need to decide before booking.
Why it works:Creates new ride demand · Increases app open rate · Gives users a reason to browse beyond commuting needs

Coursera Course Completion

A product strategy case study exploring how Coursera can increase its 18% course completion rate by 30% — through habit formation, micro-milestones, personalized learning paths, and social accountability mechanisms.

18%
Current completion rate (industry avg)
30%
Target improvement in 6 months
148M
Registered Coursera learners globally
The Problem
Despite a strong course catalog and global reach, the vast majority of learners drop off before completing the courses they enroll in — hurting learner success, retention, and certification value.
Goal
Design product interventions that build consistent learning habits, reduce overwhelm, and increase course completion rate from 18% to 23.4%.
7
Solutions proposed
6mo
Implementation timeline
Project Overview
Why learners start — and why they stop.
01
Problem Space
Research revealed predictable drop-off patterns: most learners disengage within the first 7–10 days, before building any learning momentum.
  • Only 18% of enrolled learners complete their courses
  • Most drop-offs occur within the first week
  • Learners who reach 25% completion are far more likely to finish
02
Research & Insights
Synthesized learner interviews, exit surveys, NPS feedback, community discussions, Reddit threads, and platform analytics to uncover root causes.
  • Motivation drops sharply after week one
  • Progress feels invisible without clear milestones
  • Large courses create psychological barriers
03
Strategic Pillars
Four pillars guided solution design: Motivation, Progress Visibility, Accountability, and Personalization — each targeting a different root cause of drop-off.
  • Habit-forming streaks & gamification
  • Personalized "Tailor Your Own Course" paths
  • Peer learning pods for social accountability
User Personas
Three learners, three very different journeys.
Each persona faces distinct barriers to completion — from time constraints to motivation and competing priorities.
BP
The Busy Professional
Upskilling for career growth
Goals
Upskill for promotions, switch careers, learn industry trends.
⚡ Pain: Limited time, inconsistent schedules, long learning paths cause drop-off after 2–3 weeks.
CS
The College Student
Building employability skills
Goals
Improve employability, learn beyond curriculum, explore career interests.
⚡ Pain: Competing exam priorities and multi-course enrollment leads to focus loss.
LL
The Lifelong Learner
Personal development enthusiast
Goals
Personal development, hobby exploration, and continuous learning.
⚡ Pain: Strong initial engagement but declining consistency due to lack of accountability and encouragement.
Key Insights
Five patterns that drive drop-off.
01
Most learner drop-offs begin within the first 7–10 days of enrollment.
02
Learners who reach 25% completion are significantly more likely to finish the entire course.
03
Short-term wins increase motivation more effectively than distant completion goals.
04
Peer accountability dramatically improves consistency and weekly learning retention.
05
Personalized reminders outperform generic nudges — learners respond to relevant, timely prompts.
Proposed Solutions
Seven product interventions across four strategic pillars.
Flagship · Solution 01
Tailor Your Own Course
The highest-ROI opportunity: personalized learning paths
Many learners don't need an entire course — they need specific topics. This feature lets learners browse modules individually, select what they need, and create a personalized learning path with dynamic completion tracking and time estimates. A PM, for example, can skip beginner analytics and focus only on Experimentation, SQL, and Data Storytelling.
Why it works:Reduces overwhelm · Increases relevance · Improves ownership · Accelerates time-to-value

Daily and weekly learning streaks, a habit tracker, progress calendar, and achievement badges create positive reinforcement loops that encourage learners to return consistently — transforming sporadic sessions into a daily ritual.

Key Takeaways
Five principles that guided the strategy.
🎯
Design for Behavior, Not Features
The biggest opportunity wasn't adding more content — it was understanding why users stop learning and designing against that pattern.
⚡
Small Wins Drive Big Outcomes
Breaking learning into achievable micro-milestones builds momentum that carries learners past the critical 25% threshold.
🎨
Personalization Increases Ownership
Users are significantly more likely to complete experiences they actively shape — "Tailor Your Own Course" puts agency in the learner's hands.
👥
Community Creates Accountability
People are more consistent when learning with others. Even lightweight social mechanisms like Peer Pods significantly reduce drop-off.
✨
Great Products Reduce Friction
The goal was not to make learners work harder — it was to make learning feel easier, more relevant, and worth returning to.
