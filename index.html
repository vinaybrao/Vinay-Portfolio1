WhatsApp SafeChat

A product strategy and UX case study focused on enhancing privacy in short-term WhatsApp conversations through identity masking, username-based messaging, and spam prevention mechanisms.

2B+
Monthly Active Users
91.5%

Problem
Users hesitate to interact with strangers, vendors, delivery agents, and professional contacts because WhatsApp requires sharing personal phone numbers.
Goal
Build safer, temporary, and privacy-first communication flows without sacrificing WhatsApp's simplicity.
3
Core solutions proposed
100+
Research datapoints analyzed
Project Overview
Reimagining privacy in short-term messaging interactions.
01
Problem Space
Users frequently hesitate to share their phone numbers for temporary or professional conversations. Research revealed strong concerns around spam, privacy, and unwanted follow-ups.
  • 28.2% interact with strangers weekly on WhatsApp
  • 71.8% uncomfortable sharing their number
  • 91.5% blocked contacts due to privacy concerns
02
Research & Insights
The team conducted surveys, interviews, and secondary research across platforms like Reddit, Quora, and app reviews to uncover behavioral patterns and pain points.
  • 71 survey responses
  • 15 user interviews
  • 100+ external data points analyzed
03
Solution Strategy
The proposed solutions focused on increasing privacy and reducing spam in short-term conversations without disrupting WhatsApp's core experience.
  • Masked numbers in groups
  • Username-based messaging
  • Request-to-chat approval flow
User Personas
Built around real user frustrations.
The personas highlight how professionals, students, and support teams experience privacy concerns differently when interacting with unknown contacts.

Solutions
Three privacy-focused product solutions.

Masking Numbers
Privacy-first group conversations
Users can choose who can view their phone number inside group conversations through customizable privacy controls.
